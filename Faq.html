Frequently Asked Questions

What is Scrub Typhus?

Scrub Typhus is caused by the bacteria Orientia tsutsugamushi, transmitted through infected chigger mites.

How does it spread?

It spreads through bites of infected chiggers — not from person to person.

What are the symptoms?

Fever, rash, headache, body ache, weakness, and an eschar at the bite site.

How can I prevent Scrub Typhus?

Use insect repellent, wear full clothing, avoid bushes, and inspect skin after outdoor activity.
